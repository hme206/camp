About the College

Camp Community College is a two-year institution of higher education which operates under the Virginia Community College System. Camp is comprised of two campuses (one in Franklin and the other in Suffolk), and a Center in Smithfield. The College's established service region includes the cities of Franklin and Suffolk and the counties of Isle of Wight and Southampton. The College is operated under policies established by the State Board for Community Colleges. The institution is financed with state and local funds, student tuition, and funds raised by the Paul D. Camp Community College Foundation. With service and academic excellence its primary aims, the college offers a variety of programs and services to meet the diverse needs of the community. Its two-year college transfer degree programs are designed to prepare students for transfer to four-year institutions. Its occupational and technical programs lead to associate of applied science degrees, certificates, career studies certificates and workforce credentials, and are designed to prepare individuals for certain business or technical professions. Other programs, including specialized training for industry and community service, are offered to meet the needs and interests of individuals, employers and the community. The College is operated on a year-round basis using the semester system. The availability of college credit courses in the evening, weekends, and online allows students who work to coordinate their studies and college activities with employment.
