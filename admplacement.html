Placement into Courses and Programs

Direct Enrollment

Most new students do not need to take a placement test. Camp uses a process called Direct Enrollment that places students into college courses, with appropriate pre-requisite or co-requisite academic support, if needed. Direct Enrollment is designed to help students complete their college studies in less time so that they save money, and are prepared to transfer or enter the workforce more quickly.

Multiple placement measures, such as high school GPA, SAT/ACT scores, and/or GED scores, will determine which English and mathematics courses a student needs to take. Students may be placed directly into the gateway college-level English or mathematics course required in their program of study. In some cases, direct placement into a college-level English or mathematics course may be accompanied by a requirement to co-enroll in a co-requisite course. Co-requisite courses are designed to increase student success in the accompanying college-level class. Students who are not fully prepared for direct placement into a college-level English or mathematics course, with or without the co-requisite course requirement, will be placed into a developmental course designed to elevate the student's preparedness for the gateway college-level English or mathematics course required in the student's chosen program of study. Students who graduated from high school or received a GED more than five years ago will be placed into the appropriate English and Math courses using the college's informed self-placement process.

New Students Who Completed High School Within the Last Five Years

Placement for students within five years of high school completion is based on self-reported high school GPA and the highest level of high school mathematics successfully completed; confirmed SAT, ACT, or GED scores; or previous college course grades. The charts on the following page show English and Mathematics Placement based on HS GPA, HS mathematics, SAT, ACT, and GED scores.

New Students Who Completed High School More Than Five Years Ago

Students who have been out of high school for more than five years are encouraged to discuss their education, work experience, and educational goals with an advisor in order to make an informed decision about placement. Camp will provide English and Mathematics review sheets for each student. The student will be shown examples of English and Math problems. Students will not be asked to do any writing, or solve any math problems. Rather, students will be asked whether the questions look familiar, whether they are able to complete certain types of reading and writing assignments, and have learned to solve certain types of math problems before. Based on a student's answers, they will receive a placement recommendation. The decision about whether to enroll in college-level courses, with or without a developmental pre-requisite course or co-requisite academic support course, is up to the student.

Current Students Who Have Taken Developmental Courses

In 2020-21, Camp transitioned from placement testing to Direct Enrollment. Current students who have previously taken developmental English or Mathematics courses at Camp will be advised into college courses based on a Direct Enrollment crosswalk available from your Advisor.

Placement into Calculus

The criteria for direct placement of high school graduates (less than 6 years since graduation) directly into MTH 263 – Calculus I when the VCCS pre-calculus prerequisites have not been completed, are:

  • Completion of high school Math Analysis with Trig, or Pre-Calculus with Trig with a grade of “C” or higher.
  • An overall high school GPA for 2.7 or higher.

The criteria for direct placement into MTH 263 – Calculus I for Students who graduated from high school or completed the GED or equivalent homeschool six or more years before enrolling at CAMP, may be placed directly into MTH 263 – Calculus I if they have completed high school or college math analysis or pre-calculus, with Trig, and after consultation with an advisor.

For students dual-enrolled in high school, college pre-calculus is the pre-requisite for calculus, in accordance with VCCS policy. Exceptions for students transferring into dual enrollment with Camp from a high school outside of Virginia will be considered on a case-by-case basis by the Dean of Academic Programs and Mathematics faculty.

Students Using Post-9/11/GI Bill Benefits

Students using Post-9/11/GI Bill benefits, and who are recent high school graduates, will be placed using self-reported HS GPA and highest-level high school math course; confirmed SAT, ACT, or GED scores; or previous college course grades. If a recent high school graduate is placed into pre- or co-requisite developmental courses, the student must provide either an official high school transcript or standardized test score to document placement for benefit payment. If a student cannot provide the above official documentation, the student may be directed to take the Virginia Placement Test to document placement for benefit payment.
