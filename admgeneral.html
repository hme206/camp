General Admission to the College

Individuals are eligible for admission to Camp if they are high school graduates or the equivalent, or if they are eighteen years of age or older and able to benefit academically from study at the college, as demonstrated by assessment of reading, writing, and mathematics competencies, using Direct Enrollment.

The following Virginia public school diploma types are accepted for high school equivalency: Advanced Studies, Standard, Modified Standard, General Achievement Diploma GAAHSD (formerly GAD), and General Education Development Certificates (GED). The following awards do not constitute high school graduation: Applied Studies Diploma and Certificate of Completion. Applicants for admission with these awards must complete an ability to benefit (ATB) test for admissions. These accepted and non-accepted diploma types do not apply to Home School, Out of State, Online School, or Private School applicants.

Camp reserves the right to evaluate and document special cases and to refuse or revoke admission if it is determined that the applicant or student poses a threat, is a potential danger, is significantly disruptive to the college community, or if such refusal or revocation is considered to be in the best interest of the college. Camp also reserves the right to refuse admission for applicants that have been expelled or suspended from, or determined to be a threat, potential danger, or significantly disruptive by, another college.

Individuals may be admitted to Camp as a curricular or non-curricular Student. For all curricular students, the following items are required:

  1. Completed official application for admission with social security number requested.
  2. Official transcripts from all colleges and universities attended. Graduates who complete secondary school in a home school setting must provide a graduation date and documentation of coursework. The VCCS Student Information System academic records will be sufficient for colleges within the VCCS. Official high school transcripts may be required for financial aid or benefits, competitive admission programs, and certain other college processes.
  3. Additional information as stated by Camp for admission to specific programs or curricula.

For all non-curricular students, a completed official application for admission is required with social security number requested. Non-curricular students must satisfy all required course pre-requisites or placement requirements before enrolling in specific college-level courses.
