Student Financial Aid

Mission

Paul D. Camp Community College (Camp) offers a variety of opportunities for students who need financial assistance. It is the desire of the Financial Aid Office at Camp that every qualified student has the privilege of attending college, if funds are available. The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is the first step in any assessment of financial aid eligibility for credit-based courses, including Camp scholarships. Most students who file the FAFSA receive some combination of grants, loans, scholarships and work-study funding. Filing before June 1st is strongly encouraged for the best aid package.

The Financial Aid Office has three primary functions. These include managing and disbursing local, state, and federal funds in compliance with the appropriate regulations, policies, and procedures; providing efficient and equitable services to students; and maintaining accurate records.

Since funds given are to be used for educational purposes only and limited funding is available, the college Financial Aid Office first tries to meet students' direct educational costs such as tuition, fees, books, and supplies. If there are any grant funds remaining, we then try to assist students with indirect educational costs such as transportation, personal, and living expenses. Students who feel they need more assistance to meet their educational expenses have the option to borrow a student loan.

Applying for Aid

Apply for Financial Aid. Do not lose out on free money! Nearly all students qualify to get funding to pay for school. You could too. You could qualify for grants (which you usually do not need to repay as long as you attend the full semester), loans or work-study. These can cover a good deal of your school costs. Apply early as some aid given on a first complete, first served basis. You will need to reapply each year after October 1.

  1. Get your federal financial aid FSA ID and password . This is your electronic signature. Only the person associated with the Social Security Number (SSN) tied to the FSA ID may apply for and use it. It generally takes 3-4 business days for the signature to be confirmed prior to first use.
  2. Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) with our school code (009159). Use the IRS link within the FAFSA if available to ensure accuracy. Not a citizen? State aid may be available to you through the VASA application instead.
  3. Apply to Camp online and declare an eligible major. If you already applied and wish to change majors, a Program/Plan Change Form is needed. Always check with the Financial Aid Office before changing majors to see if it will affect your financial aid.
  4. Review your ‘Student Aid Report’ which will come to your email about 24 hours after you apply online. Respond quickly to any questions and make sure Camp is listed.
  5. The Financial Aid Office will receive your information in about 3-5 business days. At that time, follow the Financial Aid Checklist for your next steps, and return any documents requested on the “To Do List” in your Student Center.
  6. While in the Student Center, check your aid notification in your Message Center for details of any special requirements for each fund. For example, if you wish to borrow a loan, additional steps are needed and you must enroll in a minimum number of credits.

Help is available in the Financial Aid Office with completing the FAFSA online during regular business hours. Visit the Camp Financial Aid web page for current contact information and hours.

Eligibility

Eligibility for federal, state, and VCCS programs is based on financial need and several other factors, as determined by responses on the (FAFSA). The Financial Aid Office staff at the college will determine eligibility. Many financial aid programs are given on a first-completed, first-served basis so it is important to apply early. Initial offerings of aid are usually based on full-time enrollment (12 or more credits). Financial aid eligibility changes with enrollment level changes. Students considering a drop or withdrawal from courses should consult a financial aid administrator before making that decision, as financial aid already received may be due back to the college.

Special and Unusual Circumstance Appeals: We understand your situation may not be accurately reflected on the FAFSA. Requests to appeal for special financial circumstances (such as a change in income since the tax year used on the FAFSA) and unusual dependency circumstances (when a student cannot provide required parental information) are considered on a case-by-case basis with adequate documentation. Financial aid forms are available online. Decisions made by financial aid administrators at other institutions are not accepted automatically and generally require additional documentation to be submitted to us as well.

Attending more than one college: Financial aid may only be paid to a student from one college per term, by federal regulation. Concurrent enrollment at another college will require the student to pay tuition and book charges out-of-pocket at one of the two colleges. However, a consortium agreement may be requested from the Financial Aid Office of the home school granting the degree after tuition has been paid to the host institution. The signed consortium form allows the home institution to award aid for classes taken at the guest college to count toward financial aid eligibility here, as long as they qualify as eligible courses at the home institution. Completed forms must be received by the Paul D. Camp College Financial Aid Office by the main refund date for the semester. Email us at finaid@pdc.edu to request a form.

Eligible Courses

Financial aid is only available for required courses for graduation when you are enrolled in a financial aid eligible program of study. A course would be deemed eligible for financial aid if all of the four following circumstances exist–

  • The course is required for graduation from your current academic program
  • The course has not already been repeated once after having been successfully completed (with any grade other than an F, I, U, R, or W)
  • The course is not below level 100 when the student has already attempted 30 or more such classes (known as developmental courses, e.g. NAS 2, MTE 10, EDE 11).
  • The course is not a basic skills class that is below the postsecondary level

Classes taken that are not required for graduation can only be considered for financial aid purposes with written documentation noting a substitution for other courses and for developmental courses. Otherwise, classes that do not meet the terms above must be paid for out-of-pocket.

Aid Determination

Depending on a number of factors, aid may be offered to a student from a mix of Federal and state grants, federal work-study, and federal student loans. Federal and state financial aid will be determined based upon your projected cost of attendance (COA) and expected family contribution (EFC), among other factors, such as residency status. If you apply for Financial Aid and are in a financial-aid approved program of study (major), you will have a COA computed for you using budgeted costs that you can reasonably expect to incur during the 9-month academic year. The COA includes direct costs such as your tuition as well as indirect costs such as your housing and transportation costs. Your EFC is determined by filing the FAFSA. Any student whose EFC is higher than the COA will not be eligible for need-based aid, such as grants, subsidized loans and work-study. Work-study eligibility also requires completion of an online Work Study Interest Form and Intent to Enroll Form . Student loans will require a separate paper application and completion of two online steps at Studentaid.gov, as noted on the loan application form. Camp scholarships are awarded in a separate online application process in fall and spring semesters; a FAFSA is required to be on file for consideration of these scholarships.

Changes in Aid

Most financial aid is offered based upon full-time enrollment, regardless of the student's level of enrollment prior to the start of classes. Aid will be reduced based on actual enrollment in eligible courses. Different types of aid have specific enrollment requirements in order to be paid. Loans and most state aid require at least 6 credits of enrollment. Pell Grants will be pro-rated down and a percentage earned for each eligible credit hour, up to 12. Enrollment in less than 12 credits as of the Last Date to Drop for a Refund (“refund date”) for the full semester may result in aid reduction or cancellation if the minimum number of credits is not met. The Financial Aid Office reserves the right to review, modify or cancel financial aid at any time on the basis of information affecting student eligibility, including changes in financial resources, enrollment status, and availability of funds. An email will be sent to notify the student of a new message in the SIS Message Center when aid has changed.

Attendance Requirement

You are only eligible to be paid the full amount for courses if you attend from the beginning to the end of each class. Starting late or withdrawing early will usually result in a reduction in aid and potentially a balance owed from the student to the college, even if refund checks have been sent out. You must enroll and attend by the refund date each semester (usually 2-3 weeks into the term) to establish eligibility, so that your class is counted for financial aid purposes. Classes added after the main refund date are ineligible for increased Pell Grant, but may qualify for a student loan. Short session classes have different refund dates, as published in the schedule, and those classes must be attended by their published refund date to qualify for aid eligibility. Dropping a late starting class after a refund has been generated will result in a balance owed back to the college. Please contact the Financial Aid Office before withdrawing or dropping a class to determine how this action may affect your financial aid and if you will owe money back.

Aid Disbursement

Financial aid can only be credited to your college account once certain conditions have been met. Financial aid funds are generally credited to your student account once per term, with the exception of single-term loans and G3 Student Success Initiative Grants, which require two disbursements. Disbursement begins approximately 40 to 60 days after the term begins. Refunds are usually processed within 14 days after disbursement. We encourage you to sign up for Direct Deposit using the email link that was sent to your college email account, for fastest refund processing.

Be careful not to count on getting your refund by a certain date to make major purchases or pay other financial obligations. Even though we issue most refunds within 14 days, it's possible your refund may require additional review and take longer. Many different factors can affect the timing of your refund after we process it. When you are anticipating a refund, please take into consideration the time it takes for your financial institution to post the refund to your account or for mail delivery. You are responsible for ensuring the “mailing address” we have on file is accurate. The U.S. Postal Service will not forward financial aid checks to a new address, and it must be returned to the College Business Office. You should cash or deposit checks within 60 days of receipt to avoid cancellation. Direct Deposit is available.

Rights in Accepting Financial Aid

You have the right to know what financial aid programs are available through the college, the application deadlines for the various financial aid programs, and to know how your financial need was determined. You have the right to request an explanation of the funds in your financial aid package, including the right to know what portion is gift assistance, loans that must be repaid, or employment. If you borrow, you have the right to know the interest rate, the terms of repayment and what may happen if you do not repay (default). You have the right to know the College's Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) policy and what happens if you do not maintain satisfactory academic progress (make the grade to keep receiving financial aid). You have the right to know the estimated cost of attendance and the College's actual tuition and fee rates, as well as the College's refund policy.

Responsibilities in Accepting Financial Aid

You are responsible for knowing catalog information. You are held individually responsible for the information contained in the College Catalog and on the financial aid webpage. Failure to read and comply with regulations will not exempt you from whatever penalties you may incur. You are responsible for attending classes, including online classes, from beginning to end. You are responsible for knowing what will happen if you withdraw from or stop attending classes, and are subject to regulations regarding the FINANCIAL AID TITLE IV REFUND and FINANCIAL AID UNOFFICIAL WITHDRAWALS REFUND POLICY. If you withdraw from the college or stop attending before more than 60% of the semester has elapsed, you should anticipate repaying a significant portion of Title IV and state financial assistance, which may exceed the amount of any refund check entitlement. You are responsible for repaying the debt if you receive aid for which you are ineligible.

Charging Books and Supplies

You are responsible for ensuring that the aid given is sufficient to pay for the classes and books charged to financial aid or paying the difference. The College reserves the right to limit the amount of allowable book and supply charges each semester, and to reject charges deemed unnecessary or over the limit. If you do not have enough aid to cover all the tuition, there will not be any aid to charge books. If you are paying Out-of-State tuition, you should realize that the federal grants are not usually enough to pay all charges, and some out-of-pocket tuition expenses are to be expected unless you choose to borrow loans. If you drop classes, you must also return corresponding books by the main refund date to avoid owing a debt for the books charged, as the aid will be reduced or cancelled. Charging of electronics requires pre-authorization after books have been charged, and other criteria have been met. Email finaid@pdc.edu with the cost of your desired electronics to receive the appropriate form with the criteria.

Repayment of Ineligible Aid

If you lose eligibility for the aid given or fail to abide by the terms and conditions to receive the funds, you must pay the tuition and bookstore charges in full upon notice from the College. You must repay any federal, state, or college and outside agency aid received as a result of unreported or misreported information discovered through verification, third-party notices, account reviews and/or audits. Dropping classes can also result in a debt to college, particularly late starting classes for which aid has already been paid and refunds issued. See FINANCIAL AID TITLE IV REFUND and FINANCIAL AID UNOFFICIAL WITHDRAWALS REFUND Policies.
