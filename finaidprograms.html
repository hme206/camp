Types of Financial Aid

Financial aid is usually available to any undergraduate who is a citizen, permanent resident, or eligible non-citizen who has completed the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA), is deemed eligible by the Department of Education, and is meeting Satisfactory Academic Progress (including enrollment in an eligible program of study). Eligible students will be packaged with aid once the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) and any required documentation have been received and processed.

Programs designated as gift aid do not normally need to be repaid, as long as a student continues to meet all attendance, academic, and other eligibility requirements. Programs designated as loan must be repaid with interest after a six-month grace period that begins when you graduate or drop below half-time. Specific Program rules are as follows:

Federal Pell Grant (PELL)

A grant provided by the federal government to undergraduate students who demonstrate exceptional financial need. A minimum of 12 credit hours each semester is required to receive full eligibility, with pro-rated per credit hour awards for students with less than 12 credit hours. Pell can be renewed as long as you make satisfactory academic progress and demonstrate sufficient financial need each year by renewing your FAFSA form. However, the amount of Federal Pell Grant funds you may receive over your lifetime is limited by federal law to be the equivalent of six years of full-time enrollment. You may only receive the Federal Pell Grant at one institution per semester.

Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)

A federal grant awarded by the institution to qualified undergraduate students who demonstrate exceptional financial need, specifically including Pell eligibility. FSEOG can be renewed as long as you make satisfactory academic progress and demonstrate sufficient financial need each year by renewing your FAFSA form and having an SAI within a given range. FSEOG funding is limited and awarded on a first-completed, first-served basis so be sure to file your FAFSA form on October 1st of each year you plan to attend school.

Students may receive from $100 to $4,000 a year depending on eligibility, funding level, residency, and the date of application. Typical amounts are between $150 and $1,500 a year. Because our funding is limited, students should apply early and turn in any required follow-up documents immediately for consideration. Federal SEOG funds are gift aid.

Federal Work-Study (FWS)

A federal program offered and administered by the institution that provides the opportunity for part-time employment to students with financial need to help pay their educational expenses. Students must fill out a separate application form and be interviewed in order to be placed into a position.

Funds are earned and paid out through a paycheck, as earned, every two weeks. These funds do not pay direct costs, such as tuition. The amount earned cannot exceed the total FWS aid posted. FWS can be renewed as long as you make satisfactory academic progress both academically and in job performance as well as by demonstrating financial need each year by renewing your FAFSA form by June 1.

Employment may be on campus or off campus (in community service organizations). The program encourages work related to the recipient's course of study. Wages for the program must equal at least the current federal minimum wage, and Camp currently pays at least $12.41/hour for all positions, which is the state minimum wage at present. Students are paid every two weeks according to actual hours worked. Working hours while school is in session is a maximum of 20 hours per week; average is 10-20 hours per week. When assigning work hours, consideration is given to the student's aid amount, class schedule, and academic progress. Interested students must complete an online Work-Study Interest Form and Intent to Enroll Form.

State Commonwealth Grant (COMA)

The Virginia Commonwealth Grant (COMA) is a source of state aid for undergraduate Virginia residents who demonstrate financial need. Aid amounts cannot exceed tuition and required fees, and are usually $1,400-$2,064 per year (no books allowed). Student must enroll for 6 or more eligible credits to qualify for your COMA award. Camp students taking 15 or more credits who meet other eligibility requirements will qualify for additional Commonwealth grant, so they can finish sooner (15 to Finish!). COMA can be renewed as long as you make satisfactory academic progress (SAP) and demonstrate financial need each year by renewing your FAFSA form. Commonwealth grant funding is limited and awarded on a first-completed, first-served basis so be sure to file your FAFSA form by June 1 of each year you plan to attend school. For complete details about COMA, visit the State Council of Higher Education (SCHEV) website.

State Virginia Guaranteed Assistance Program (VGAP)

The Virginia Guaranteed Assistance Program (VGAP) requires full-time (12 or more credits) enrollment each term, 15 preferred. Camp students taking 15 or more credits who meet other eligibility requirements will qualify for additional VGAP grant, so they can finish sooner (15 to Finish!). This aid is only available to domiciled Virginia residents. VGAP and COMA may not be given during same term, so a student will receive the higher award for which they are eligible. First year VGAP amounts range from $2,000 up to $5,400 and are available to undergraduate Virginia residents who graduated from a Virginia high school with at least a 2.5 grade point average. Students must submit a final high school transcript to the Financial Aid Office for initial consideration. Home school completers will also need to submit ACT or SAT scores to the Financial Aid Office. For renewal consideration (one additional year only), you must file a FAFSA by June 1, continue to meet income guidelines, maintain a 2.0 cumulative GPA and complete 30 credits. If you plan to transfer to a 4-year school, you submit proof of VGAP at Camp to the Financial Aid Office at your transfer school for two additional years. The range of second year grants are generally $50-100 higher, according to SAI range.

Part Time Tuition Assistance Program (PTAP)

A locally-funded grant, which provides tuition assistance to undergraduate Virginia residents who demonstrate financial need. Aid amounts cannot exceed tuition and fees per academic year (no books). A minimum of 1 credit hour and a maximum of 11 credit hours are required in order to receive PTAP. This grant is typically not given as part of the initial aid package, as that is based on projected full-time enrollment. It is awarded during the repackaging process when aid is based on actual enrollment. Limited funds are available, so they are usually given to students who had COMA or other aid that must be cancelled due to enrollment in less than 6 credits. PTAP and COMA may not be given during the same term, so a student will usually only receive PTAP only if not meeting COMA requirements. The minimum award is $50.00 and the maximum is actual eligible tuition, up to 11 credits and books.

G3 Grant

The G3 grant provides last dollar financial assistance to students who are domiciliary residents of Virginia in specific occupational/technical programs of study who lack sufficient grant aid to cover tuition, fees, and books*. Eligible students must have a total household income of less than or equal to 400% of the Federal Poverty Level (roughly an income of $100,000 for a family of four) and be enrolled in only G3-eligible programs. Last dollar funds will cover up to actual tuition charges minus other grants and third-party payments (not loans or scholarships), for eligible credits. *The book stipend is $250 for half-time enrollment, $375 for ¾ time, and $500 for full-time. For complete details about the G3 program visit SCHEV's website.

G3 Student Success Initiative Grant (SSIG)

The SSIG grant is awarded to students enrolled in G3-eligible program(s) of study, domiciled in Virginia, enrolled in a minimum of 12 credits and also awarded a full-time Pell award. Must have a total household income of less than or equal to 400% of the Federal Poverty Level (roughly an income of $100,000 for a family of four). Student can only be enrolled in G3 eligible programs of study. This grant is $900, which is disbursed half after census date and half after grades post at the end of the semester, if still enrolled full-time.

Virginia Community College System (VCCS) Grant

The VCCS Grant provides financial assistance to Virginia residents at Virginia's Community Colleges who demonstrate unmet financial need. Award amounts cannot exceed tuition, required fees, and a book allowance. This grant is not awarded as part of the initial aid package. It may be awarded during the repackaging process that takes place after the end of the add/drop period where aid is based on actual enrollment and in special circumstances, as determined by the Financial Aid Coordinator.

Federal Direct Stafford Loan

Federal loan funds that you must repay with interest. Repayment begins 6 months after you cease to be enrolled at least half-time. For subsidized loans, the government pays the interest on the loan while you remain enrolled at least half-time and during certain periods when the government allows deferment of repayment. For unsubsidized loans, interest accrual begins immediately and you can choose to pay the interest while enrolled or upon entering repayment.

For either type of loan, you must complete Entrance Counseling and a Master Promissory Note (MPN) to receive these loan funds if you have not done so already. To qualify for each disbursement, you must be enrolled for 6 or more credits in an eligible program of study. There are annual limits on the amounts that may be borrowed, which vary by your academic year in school and your dependent or independent status. Eligible students borrow directly from the U.S. Department of Education. You are encouraged to borrow only what you need to meet your educational expenses. Not all VCCS colleges participate in the federal loan programs. The college reserves the right to reduce or deny a loan request on a case-by-case basis under 34 CFR 685.301(a) when a student a) has a pattern of non-passing grades, b) has a previous default, or c) has an existing loan debt that approaches undergraduate limits.

Types of Loans Include:

Subsidized: for students with demonstrated financial need, as determined by federal regulations. The federal government “subsidizes” the interest, meaning no interest is charged while a student is in school at least half-time, during the grace period, and during deferment periods.

Unsubsidized: not based on financial need; interest is charged quarterly during all periods, even during the time a student is in school and during grace and deferment periods. If unpaid, interest is compounded, meaning the student will accrue interest on the previously unpaid interest after leaving school.

PLUS: unsubsidized loans for the parents of dependent students. PLUS loans help pay for education expenses up to the cost of attendance minus all other financial assistance. Interest is charged during all periods.

Loan limits: There is a maximum amount you can borrow each year in Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans which depends on your grade level and on whether you are a dependent student or an independent student. Refer to the Department of Education's Loan website, our Student Loan Program website, or the chart on the bottom of our Federal Direct Student Loan Request Form for more information. Please note the loan limit changes coming after July 1, 2026 so you can plan ahead.

Please note: Loans are always disbursed in two parts. Loans requested for Fall and Spring at the same time are paid out in one disbursement per term. Loans requested for a single term at a time (Fall, Spring or Summer) are disbursed in two halves, and the second disbursement must occur after the midpoint of the semester. The Financial Aid Office rechecks enrollment and SAP status prior to each disbursement. At least half-time enrollment must be maintained in order for each disbursement to occur.

Other Aid: Other aid may become available as a result of state or federal action. Aid will be awarded to eligible students in accordance with all applicable legislation, policy, and procedures.

Institutional Aid: Scholarship Assistance

The Office of Institutional Advancement manages scholarships for the college. More information on each scholarship is listed on the college website. Filing a FAFSA is required for all Camp scholarships except DECOS and Workforce Development scholarships.

Other Assistance

Federal Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant: A student who is not eligible for a Pell Grant but whose parent or guardian was a member of the U.S. Armed Forces and died as a result of service performed in Iraq or Afghanistan after September 11, 2001 may be eligible to receive the Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant. Eligible students must be under 24 years old or enrolled in college at least part-time at the time of the parent's or guardian's death. The grant award is equal to the amount of a maximum Pell Grant for the aid year – not to exceed the cost of attendance for that aid year.

Federal Children of Fallen Heroes Scholarship: Under this scholarship, a Pell-eligible student whose parent or guardian died in the line of duty while performing as a public safety officer is eligible to receive a maximum Pell Grant for the aid year for which the determination of eligibility is made. To qualify for this scholarship, a student must be Pell-eligible and be less than 24 years of age or enrolled at an institution of higher education at the time of his or her parent's or guardian's death. In subsequent aid years, the student continues to be eligible for the scholarship, as long as the student has a Pell-eligible EFC and continues to be an eligible student. All federal aid awarded to such eligible students must be based on an EFC of zero without regard to the student's calculated EFC. Use EFC eligibility to determine who is considered a public safety officer in order to qualify for this scholarship.

VCCS Foster Care Tuition Grant: The Tuition Grant program provides tuition and fees at any Virginia community college for high school graduates or general education development (GED) completers in foster care, in the custody of a social services agency, or considered a special needs adoption. A minimum of 6 credit hours is required and financial need must be demonstrated. The grant covers tuition and fees only for students not already receiving sufficient federal and state grant eligibility to cover those expenses. Students who would like to be considered for this grant should send proof of their foster care or special needs adoptee status to the Financial Aid Office.
